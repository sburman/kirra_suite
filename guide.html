Local Guide

Your guide to Kirra & Coolangatta

Everything within a stroll or a short drive of The Kirra Suite — the surf, the sand, and where the locals eat and drink.

The beach & the surf

Kirra is one of Australia's most iconic north-facing beaches — a long arc of soft sand with a legendary point break that has drawn surfers for generations. When the swell lines up it produces some of the best waves on the coast; when it's calm, it's a gentle, family-friendly swim. The patrolled flags, the surf club and the scenic oceanfront pathway are all just across the road from the apartment.

Walk south and you'll reach Coolangatta, Greenmount and the famous Snapper Rocks and Rainbow Bay — the start of the Superbank, one of the longest rideable waves in the world. Point Danger and the Captain Cook Memorial mark the New South Wales border, with sweeping views back over the coastline.

Eat & drink nearby

You barely need to go far — a handful of relaxed spots sit within an easy walk:

  • Kirra Beach Hotel — a relaxed pub serving Australian classics.
  • Kirra Beach House — beachfront casual dining with an ocean outlook.
  • Made Espresso Bar — specialty coffee for a laid-back breakfast or brunch.

A little further along, the Coolangatta strip adds more cafés, restaurants, bakeries and beachfront bars — an easy walk for dinner or a sunset drink.

Getting here

The Kirra Suite is on Marine Parade, Kirra (Coolangatta) QLD 4225. Gold Coast Airport (OOL) is roughly a ten-minute drive away, making it one of the easiest beachfront stays to reach on the coast. Your stay includes one complimentary secure car space, and a private storage cage in the car park is ideal for surfboards, bicycles and e-bikes.
